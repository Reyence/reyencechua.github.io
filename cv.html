Research Experience

Measuring Structural Complexity with Combinatorial-Topological Entropy
Independent Research / Preprints (2025)
2025
  • Introduced Combinatorial-Topological Entropy (CTE), a structural measure quantifying the intrinsic complexity of combinatorial topologies, including simplicial complexes and hypergraphs.
  • CTE uses simplex dimensions, adjacency hierarchies, and connectivity patterns rather than probability distributions.
  • Formalized a version incorporating parameters α and β to weight simplex size and adjacency influence.
  • Illustrative examples (tetrahedra, hypergraphs, higher-dimensional simplicial complexes) demonstrate sensitivity to structural features and distinguish between combinatorial configurations.
  • Visualized trends via heatmaps showing adjacency and size effects.
